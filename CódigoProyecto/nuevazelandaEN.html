Some points of interest:

1. A river of blue pools in New Zealand (Blue Pool Track) Blue Pool Track, is an interesting corner to meet on a walk along the west coast of New Zealand. In the middle of a native beech forest, the Makarora River surprises by its transparent waters crossing a rocky bed that after thousands of years of erosion gives rise to a series of pools and pools in turquoise

2. A beach with spherical rocks, Koekohe In New Zealand, there is a beach dotted with spherical rocks. They are known as Moeraki Boulders, on Koekohe Beach, New Zealand. They look like mysterious giant eggs that are nothing more than the result of a patient erosion over millions of years, from the waves and the sand on rocks with high component of calcite.
