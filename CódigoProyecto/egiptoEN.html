What to see:

1. Abu Simbel or The Great Temple of Ramses II on the shores of Lake Nasser And there are no words to describe it ... incredible, surprising, enigmatic ... Legend has it that in the thirteenth century BC. Ramses II command to build the two incredible temples that make up Abu Simbel ... The first of them, the one of Ramses II itself, was dedicated to the gods Amón, Ra-Horajti and Ptah, as well as deified Ramses. The facade of the temple is 33 meters high and is guarded by four colossal statues of Ramses II carved in the rock. Each of them is about twenty meters high!

2. The Pyramids of Giza The pyramids of Giza: the most iconic legacy of Egyptian civilization, the only survivor of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World and, without a doubt, the greatest symbol of Egypt!
