Waterfalls of Hierve el Agua or El Nevado de Toluca

Relax zones of the hotel

What to see:

1. Waterfalls of Hierve el Agua, Mitla Valley, Oaxaca Hierve el Agua is a system of petrified waterfalls, formed by calcium carbonate. The waterfalls are of natural origin and were formed thousands of years ago by the water runoff with high mineral content. The site is located about 50 km from the city of Oaxaca, in the town of San Isidro Roaguía, municipality of San Lorenzo Albarradas, state of Oaxaca, Mexico, near the archaeological site of Mitla and 630 km from the city Of Mexico.

2. The Nevado de Toluca, in the State of Mexico The Nevado de Toluca or Xinantécatl is a Mexican volcano located in the state of Mexico, between the valleys of Toluca and Tenango (Matlatzinco Valley). It is located 22 km southwest of Toluca, State of Mexico. It reaches an elevation of 4680 msnm, being the fourth highest formation of Mexico and forming part of the Transversal Neovolcanic Cordillera and of the Belt of Fire of the Pacific.
